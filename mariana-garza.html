Mariana Garza Career

Mariana Garza, the daughter of Ana Silvia Garza and granddaughter of Mexican poet Carmen Alardín and Ramiro Garza, has had a diverse and successful career. Her journey began in 1979 with her debut work, “Elisa,” she ventured into theater in the play “La Maravilla de crecer” the following year. However, her breakthrough came in 1982 when she joined the popular band “Timbiriche.” Despite the band’s immense success, Mariana left in 1987 to focus on her acting career.

Throughout her career, Mariana has graced the screens with her talent, featuring in soap operas such as “Flor y Canela” and achieving great recognition with “Alcanzar Una Estrella.” She also served as a TV host on the well-received Mexican program “Mi Barrio” for nearly two years.

In her personal life, Mariana is married to actor Pablo Perroni, and she is parents to two daughters, Shamadhi (from her first marriage) and Maria. In 2001, she released her only solo album, “Todo Tiene Tambor,” although her most enduring hit remains the theme song “Alcanzar una Estrella.”

Mariana’s versatility extends to her theater, television, and music work. In 2013, she co-starred in “Mentir para Vivir,” portraying Maria Jiménez Flores, Sebastián’s mother, in a challenging role. Furthermore, in 2017, Mariana reunited with the original founding members of Timbiriche to celebrate the group’s 35th anniversary, continuing with a tour into 2018. Her career is a testament to her multifaceted talents and enduring presence in the entertainment industry.
