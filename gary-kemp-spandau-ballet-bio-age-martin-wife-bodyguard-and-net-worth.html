Gary Kemp Biography

Gary Kemp is a celebrated English Singer, Songwriter, Musician, Actor and Guitarist who was born and brought-up in Smithfield, London. He is widely known as the lead guitarist, backing vocalist and principal songwriter for ‘Spandau Ballet’, the New Romantic Wave band. He won the ‘Ivor Novello Award winner for Outstanding Song Collection’ in 2012.

Gary wrote the lyrics and music for all of Spandau Ballet’s hit singles, which are 23 in total including “To Cut a Long Story Short”, “Gold”, “Chant No. 1, I Don’t Need This Pressure On” and “Only When You Leave”. He has also appeared on notable films and series including ‘The Bodyguard’, ‘Molly Moon and the Incredible Book of Hypnotism’ and ‘American Daylight’.

Gary Kemp Spandau Ballet

Gary is the lead guitarist, backing vocalist, and principal songwriter for the New Romantic band ‘Spandau Ballet’. In 1989, Kemp and his brother, Martin returned to acting after recording Spandau Ballet’s final album Heart Like a Sky. His fellow band members Tony Hadley, Steve Norman and John Keeble criticized him but received good reviews for his performance as Ronnie Kray in The Krays (1990).

On March 6, 1990, Spandau played the final live show of their 10th-anniversary tour at the Edinburgh Playhouse. The band did not perform live together again for 19 years. His fellow band members Tony Hadley, Steve Norman and John Keeble attempted to sue him for alleged unpaid royalties in 1999.
