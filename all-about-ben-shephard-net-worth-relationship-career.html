How did Ben Shephard start his Professional Career?

Shephard gained notoriety in 1998 when he co-hosted The Bigger Breakfast, a Channel 4 spin-off program, with Josie D’Arby, Melanie Sykes, and Dermot O’Leary. Shephard served as T4’s inaugural host when it premiered on October 25, 1998, on Channel 4.

He also broadcast Control Freaks on the channel in 1998. Shephard made a comeback to Channel 4 in 2013 to co-host the food program What’s Food? He hosted one show alongside Lisa Faulkner before leaving Channel 4 permanently on May 24, 2013.

While Kate Thornton hosted the ITV main show between 2004 and 2006, Shephard presided over the first three seasons of the ITV2 spin-off The Xtra Factor. Shephard not only hosted but also participated in The Xtra Factor – Battle of the Stars.

He left the show after three seasons and was replaced by Fearne Cotton after being passed up for the primary hosting position (which went to Dermot O’Leary after the departure of original host Kate Thornton). He served as This Morning’s replacement for regular presenter Phillip Schofield from 2005 to 2011.

Shephard and Fern Britton shared hosting duties for the reality singing contest Soapstar Superstar in 2006. Shephard co-hosted the ITV spinoff show Dancing on Ice Exclusive in 2007. In 2000, Shephard started anchoring Entertainment Today alongside Jenni Falconer on GMTV.
