Jairek Robbins- Professional Career

While still in school, he participated in the Semester at Sea program and spent a semester abroad in Japan, China, India, Thailand, Vietnam, Tanzania, Brazil, South Africa, and Venezuela. In an interview with Forbes, he admitted that this inspired much of his work and concentration on finding a way to make a positive change in the world through volunteerism and philanthropy.

He began his career at the Anthony Robbins Foundation in 2002, where he worked for two years. During college, he lived in a remote agricultural hamlet for months and volunteered to teach organic farming in Uganda. Furthermore, he served on the board of “Just Like My Child,” a non-profit organization that also conducts health and education programs in Uganda.

Robbins is also a regular contributor to the Huffington Post blog and hosts a weekly YouTube program called JRCtv. He gave a TEDx lecture in New York City in 2013 titled ‘A Simple Formula To Inspire the World To Live Their Dreams.’

Later, he worked as a Results Coach for Robbins Research International. From 2006 to 2008, he worked as a Results Coach representative for Robbins International. He won a Congressional Award Gold Medal in 2008. It is a non-competitive prize given to young Americans who have participated in activities such as volunteer public service. He created Jairek Robbins Companies in 2010, and Rapid Results Retreat a year after.
