When Was Nate Archibald Born? Early Life And Family

Shortly after Nathaniel “Tiny” Archibald, Nate Archibald was born in New York City, New York, to his parents, Julia Archibald and Big Tiny (nicknamed) Archibald.

He was the second son among six children and grew up in New York’s South Bronx. Within the glamour city of New York lies a place manifested with gangs, bullets, and violence.

The place gives rise to murders and drugs as if nothing happened, and the two-winged dreams get crushed into the ground. However, Nate could control it all, finding his escape in the basketball.

As he grew from a child to a teenager, he commenced shooting balls in the basket at a concrete, uneven playground. The determination that he carried from his teenage phase to school gets crushed one day.

Education

During his tenure in the DeWitt Clinton High School, he had only contributed one and a half-season for his school’s team until he was cut out.

Being a sophomore, he had to run with the junior teams, which gave birth to the idea of giving up. At that moment, his two mentors, Floyd Layne and Pablo Robertson, were the ones to show him the path of encouragement. 

To illustrate, they were the former standout at Loyola of Chicago and Harlem. Thus, Archibald gave another chance at basketball and got reinstated on the squad.

In addition, the shy Archibald played a transition season at tiny Arizona Western College, where he rose as a high school star with an average of 29.5 points per game.

Right then, he began to work on his academics and transferred to the University of Texas at El Paso (UTEP). In UTEP, he stood as the point guard, where he worked under Hall of fame coach Don Haskins.

From 1967 to 1970, Archibald maintained attention towards him, especially since his 51-point performance in the 1970 Aloha Classic.

Nate Archibald | Professional Career

After College graduation, Archibald entered the 1970 NBA Draft, where the Cincinnati Royals selected Nate Archibald in the second round as the 19th overall pick.

Cincinnati Royals/Kansas City Kings

Archibald debuted against the New York Knicks with The City Kings, where he posted 17 points and seven assists.

Following it, Archibald became the only player to win the titles in scores and assists in the 1972–73 season.

Back then, Nate surpassed the NBA record for a guard with his average of 34.0 points per game, which is valid until today.

Additionally, his 910 assists were the record-breaker for the NBA, leaving behind Guy Rodgers. In addition to that, Nate was titled the Sporting News NBA MVP.

New York Nets

With internal management problems, the Kings traded Archibald to the New York Nets. However, he couldn’t play much for the team as he struggled with a foot injury, ending with 34 games.

Buffalo Braves

As his injury was prolonged, the Nets traded Archibald to the Buffalo Braves. However, he couldn’t officially play the matches. During the opening, he tore his Achilles tendon, which kept him away from the games.

Boston Celtics

Later, the Buffaloes traded Archibald to the Celtics, where he had a poor start. Archibald was 20 pounds overweight during the tenure and was told to lose it, which helped him throughout the season.

Playing with the Celtics, Archibald had his first and only NBA championship in the 1980–81 season, where he paired with young NBA star Larry Bird.

“Some guys have had a much better career than myself and haven’t won a championship. You look at players like Charles Barkley and Patrick Ewing and realize how lucky you were to win it all. I was able to persevere through the injuries and keep learning the game. I was the facilitator on a championship team. To do it with the Boston Celtics is a dream come true and something I’ll cherish for the rest of my life.”
-Nate Archibald

Post-NBA

During his NBA career, Nate Archibald took his bachelor’s degree parallelly from the University of Texas-El Paso.

After he closed his basketball career, Archibald commenced teaching in the New York City school system and then at Fordham University.

In 1990, Archibald earned his master’s degree from Fordham University, followed by his professional diploma in supervision and administration.

Additionally, Archibald dreamt of having a doctorate through California Coast University in the future at Fordham.

All in all, he dropped it off after he found his lack of funds and motivation to continue his work on it.

Moreover, Nate also became the assistant coach at the University of Georgia for a season while he spent two seasons with Texas El Paso.

Equally important, he coached for various institutions such as New Jersey Jammers of the USBL and New Jersey Jammers of the USB.

Likewise, he has also coached in the National Basketball Development League. He also stood as the head coach for the Long Beach Jam in 2004.

Nate Archibald | Awards And Highlights

Archibald spent fourteen years of life in the NBA, where he had set his name for the long run. We will discuss the career highlights and achievements in brief.

Until today, Archibald became the All-NBA First Team selection three times in 1973, 1975, and 1976 while he was the All-NBA Second Team selection two times in 1972 and 1981.

Later, when he was the 1981 NBA All-Star Game MVP, he also appeared seven times in the NBA All-Star Game selection.

Overall, Nate Archibald had played 876 professional games, where he posted 16,841 points (18.8 points per game) and 6,476 assists.

Furthermore, he was the NBA leader in free throws three times, while he entered the NBA’s 50th Anniversary All-Time team.

Nate Archibald | Heart Transplant Surgery

Archibald had a successful year in the NBA; however, he ended with a tragic event at 69. Archibald was diagnosed with amyloidosis, an incurable condition due to amyloid building in the organs.

When Archibald became aware of his situation, he underwent tests for confirmation, and since it had no cure, the only option was a transplant.

In the meantime, the doctors treating him made him aware of his situation stating that he could go any time.

Subsequently, Archibald underwent a cardiac rehabilitation program before surgery and took in a strict diet. As Nate recalls,

“I took their horse pills, I ate their nasty food. It was like playing hoops to me. I was trying to get in a zone. I wanted to be left alone.”

Altogether, Archibald took 26 days with his surgery and treatments as his recovery came in remarkably.
