Career

In 2013, Grayson and his brother, Ethan, became famous on a video sharing application called Vine. They have over 6.4 million followers on Vine. In March 2014, Grayson and his brother, Ethan, started their YouTube channel by the name ‘Dolan Twins.’ The first video they uploaded was ‘The Blender Challenge’ in June 2015.

They have over 11.1 million followers on their YouTube channel.

In 2015, after they signed with William Morris Endeavor agency, they moved to Los Angeles. On March 27, 2018, Grayson and Ethan, announced their hiatus from YouTube through a video on their channel, which was titled ‘Bye For Now.’ In the video, they talked about how their work has changed with time and they wanted to explore themselves creatively. On May 1, 2018, they announced their return. In June 2018, the twins, James Charles (YouTuber), and Emma Chamberlain (YouTuber) formed a group called ‘The Sister Squad’ on YouTube. The squad uploaded videos of themselves on their channels and were featured prominently in YouTube Rewind 2018.

The Sister Squad

On October 8, 2019, they uploaded a sixty-minute video titled ‘It’s Time To Move On…’ in which they (Grayson and Dolan) explained the stress of their father’s death. In the video, they also announced that they plan to change the content of their channel and have more flexible schedules.

Controversies

  • In 2018, many of their (Grayson and Ethan) fans who are people of color shared times when they received racist remarks from other people in the fandom and requested Dolan Twins to address the issue. After the news reached them, Ethan tweeted that they were all the same. The response of Ethan disappointed their fans and they called out to them (Dolan twins) as they (fans) believed that he was not addressing the issue directly but avoiding it. After the backlash, Ethan tweeted that they do not tolerate racism and that he was not avoiding the issue. He further stated that whoever was discriminating against the people on the bases of color, ethnicity, or race, should unfollow him. [2]
  • In 2020, some fans of Dolan Twins trended ‘#DolanTwinsAreOverParty’ after an old clip of them surfaced online in which they seemingly used homophobic language. In an old YouTube video, Grayson and Ethan were discussing the bullying that they faced during their high school. Grayson recollected things that people had posted about him online, which also included homophobic slurs. People took a short clip of Grayson saying homophobic slurs (from the video) and spread it around online, due to which, Grayson faced a huge backlash. [3]
