Brandon Jennings is a retired American professional basketball player. He has played for a variety of teams in the National Basketball Association. Jennings is the first non-college-educated professional athlete in the United States. From 2008 through 2018, he was employed in his field.

Where was Brandon Jennings born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

He went to Dominguez High School before attending Oak Hill Academy. Throughout his academic career, he received numerous important awards. He did, however, forego education and became the Euroleague’s first player to play professionally without a college diploma.

How did Brandon Jennings start his Professional Career?

Brandon Jennings was on his way to Southern California University. Lottomatica Roma, a Serie A club in Italy, signed him in 2018. In the 2018-19 season, he averaged 5.5 points and 1.6 rebounds. In 16 Euroleague appearances, Jennings averaged 7.6 points per game.

He then entered the 2009 NBA Draft, where he was selected by the Milwaukee Bucks. He scored 17 points in his NBA debut against the Philadelphia 76ers in October 2009.

In November 2009, he also scored 55 points against the Golden State Warriors. His deal with the Milwaukee Bucks expired in 2013. He was eventually traded to the Detroit Pistons, where he signed a three-year contract. He had his best season of his career in 2015, with 37 points. In 2016, he was traded to the Orlando Magic once more.

In contrast, his time with the Orlando Magic was brief. He signed with the New York Knicks in 2016. He made his NBA debut in October 2016, scoring 7 points against the Cleveland Cavaliers. After a year with the New York Knicks, Jennings was signed by the Washington Wizards in 2017.

Following that, he elected to join the Shanxi Brave Dragons of the Chinese Basketball Association in 2017. He later joined the NBA G League’s Wisconsin Herd. In 2018, he returned to the Milwaukee Bucks and signed a 10-day contract with them.

Jennings was then recruited by Zenit Saint Petersburg of the VTB United League. Despite signing in August 2018, he only appeared in ten games before being released in November. Jennings appeared in 555 regular-season games during his NBA career.
