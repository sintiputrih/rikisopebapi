Jesseca Dupart Net Worth – $8 million

What is Jesseca Dupart’s net worth?

Introduction

Jesseca Dupart, also known socially as “Judy” to her supporters, is an American entrepreneur best known for her hair product company Kaleidoscope Hair Products.

Biography

She was born on February 12, 1982, in New Orleans, Louisiana, USA.

Her father is Anthony Dupart, a realtor who had a brief stint in the mayor’s office, and her mother is Evelyn Dupart.

Jessica’s father passed away of congestive heart failure, aged 72, on 13th January 2011.

She has three siblings, two brothers and a sister named; Jesse Jr. Dupart, Damon Sr. Dupart, and Jheri Dupart.

Dupart has been doing hair since she was a child.

At age 7, she was styling the hair on her dolls. Five years later, Jesseca was braiding her friends’ hair after school.

”My mom always tells me stories about how I used to put barrettes in my dad’s hair, so it was something that I always wanted to pursue,” she said for Hype Hair. ”Although, I didn’t realize it would turn into all of this.”

She graduated from Warren Easton Charter High School.

Kaleidoscope Hair Products

@Getty

Unfortunately, a raging fire gutted the studio and shattered her aspirations.

But Jessica isn’t one to give up or lose hope. She picked up the broken pieces of her destroyed studio and established another enterprise, Kaleidoscope Hair Products.

The business’s retail products gained the approval and patronage of numerous customers. It also earned her several endorsements from well-known brands and names.

Brimming with passion and ambition, Dupart founded her hair care company in 2016 and has over 43,000 clients at present. She later launched her website, www.iluvcolors.com.

In early 2018, Kaleidoscope sales reached $100k per month.

”I was a hairstylist for four years,” she said on Breakfast Club Power in April 2019. ”I decided to dip into hair products … I was able to make well over fifteen million dollars in a few years.”

In 2019, Dupart was awarded the Key to the City of New Orleans by LaToya Cantrell — the mayor of New Orleans. She said at the time: ”I am filled with inspiration and motivation to continue to carry this light and propel other leaders to shine in the darkness no matter the obstacle.”

On March 31, 2019, Jesseca penned the book – ”When The Miracle Drops: How Instagram Helped Turn A Quick Fix Into A Million-Dollar Product.”

Partner

”I’ve always felt like being private is the better way to go because then you don’t have so many people in your business,” Da Brat confessed in an interview with Variety. She continued: ”I was fine staying quiet, but my partner is a social media mogul.”

”I love the fact that she has an amazing heart, a beautiful heart, and it fills her soul to make other people happy and to give back,” Da Brat said about Jesseca for Advocate. ”She will give the shirt off her back to anyone, she gives free million-dollar advice to people, she just wants to help people and see them grow and prosper.”

”You’re more than I could’ve ever imagined,” Jesseca wrote on her Instagram in April 2021. ”The type of selfless love you show me is indescribable. You are everything to me. I can’t imagine life without you and never want to.”

In February 2022, they married in Georgia.

Children

Dupart has three children.

Dupart welcomed her first child at the young age of 15 and had another at 17.

Her second pregnancy reportedly caused a rift between her and her family, forcing her to become independent at a tender age.

”I had my kids at 15, 17, and 19, and I miscarried twins between 17 and 19,” Jesseca told Beauty Independent in September 2020. She added: ”I was going to a top-rated school at the time of my first pregnancy, and they had never had a pregnant student there.”

So, how much is Jesseca Dupart worth?

Dupart earned most of her wealth from founding and managing Kaleidoscope Hair Products. The products are sold in Walmart, Sally Beauty, and Target.

Celebrities who have promoted the line include – Christina Millian, RHOA’s Nene Leakes, and Blac Chyna.

In March 2018, Kaleidoscope Hair Products reached $1 million in monthly sales.

”I bought a Bentley, not because I like cars,” Dupart said for Wired. ”I couldn’t care less about them, but because it got respect and people listened to me. I got it for the purpose it served.”

Dupart also makes money from selling copies of her book When The Miracle Drops.

In 2020, she founded a real estate agency, Kaleidoscope Realty, where she manages a multi-million dollar commercial property.

Dupart is also a celebrated philanthropist who enjoys making charitable donations. She has donated a large sum of money to the Bardell foundation to support the fight against breast cancer. The Guinness Book of World Records has her company listed for giving out the largest number of gifts in a day.

Therefore, American entrepreneur Jesseca Dupart has an estimated net worth of $8 million.
